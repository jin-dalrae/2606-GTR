Embedding carbon accountability where founders already show up

One step inside accelerator onboarding — not a new voluntary program — so EI reaches every founder, every cohort, by default.

Systems change
05 stages
Rae · Gabriel · Tej

Instead of asking individual startups to voluntarily measure impact, the assessment rides inside an accelerator’s existing intake — so it reaches every founder, in every cohort, by default. One embedded step propagates the practice without depending on any single founder’s initiative.

Stage 01

Pitch to program directors

Introduce the work to program directors at climate accelerators — as a way to strengthen shared founder–investor understanding of climate impact.

Buy-in at the top, once.

Stage 02

Founder onboarding

A climate-specific accelerator adds a ~2-minute carbon assessment as a standard step in existing application & onboarding — no new curriculum slot required.

One accelerator adopts the standard.

Stage 03

Identify current progress

Founders see their company’s footprint / handprint generated. Program directors view the entire cohort on one platform at the same time.

Every founder, every cohort — automatically.

Stage 04

Prep for Demo Day

Founders build metrics into their pitch. PDs send investors a link before Demo Day — compiling the cohort’s assessment results in one consistent format.

One format across the cohort.

Stage 05

Communicate with investors

Founders present with metrics. Investors see EI across every pitch — and begin to expect it in future cohorts.

Normalized across the investment pipeline.
