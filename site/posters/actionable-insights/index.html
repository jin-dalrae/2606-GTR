An effective EI dashboard (like the one used in GTR Partners) doesn’t just show numbers — it helps founders make better decisions. Here are the key types of actionable insights early-stage startups typically get:

  • Which part of your business contributes the most to your environmental impact (e.g., cloud computing, supplier manufacturing, shipping, office operations, or product usage).
  • Specific suppliers or materials that are driving high emissions.

ActionPrioritize which area to tackle first instead of guessing.

03

Scenario & Decision Support

  • Compare the environmental impact of different choices before making them, such as:
  • Choosing between two suppliers
  • Selecting different packaging or shipping methods
  • Deciding between cloud providers or data centers

ActionMake lower-impact decisions during product development and operations.

07

Resource Prioritization

  • Clear view of which initiatives will deliver the biggest environmental improvement relative to effort and cost.
  • Helps small teams avoid wasting time on low-impact activities.

ActionFocus limited resources on changes that actually move the needle.

  • Your company’s EI per dollar of revenue compared to industry averages or similar-stage startups.

04

Progress Tracking Against Targets

EI intensity over time →

  • Clear visibility into whether you’re on track to meet your EI reduction goals.
  • Month-over-month or quarter-over-quarter trends in your EI intensity.

ActionKnow when to double down on initiatives or change strategy.

06

Early Risk Signals

  • Early warnings if certain activities (e.g., rapid scaling, new suppliers, or entering new markets) are significantly increasing your environmental impact.
  • Identification of potential future regulatory or customer risks.

ActionAddress issues before they become expensive problems.

Band C · Story

Investors and the whole team

05 · 08
05

Fundraising & Investor-Ready Insights

  • Clean, credible data to show investors how your environmental performance is improving.
  • Ability to demonstrate that you’re managing risk and building a more resilient business.

ActionUse real numbers in pitch decks and investor updates instead of vague statements.

08

Internal Alignment & Culture

  • Simple visuals that make it easy to explain environmental performance to the whole team.
  • Helps turn EI from “one person’s job” into a shared team responsibility.

ActionBuild a culture where environmental impact is discussed alongside growth metrics.
