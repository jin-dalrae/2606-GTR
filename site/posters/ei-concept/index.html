How Environmental Impact is calculated and used?

Measure, normalize, and compare across three scales — then roll company data into capital and policy.

Team
Rae · Gabriel · Tej
EI · Environmental Impact

The total load — e.g. tCO₂e from Scope 1–2–3 (extendable to water, land, waste). Absolute tonnes — not yet fair across company sizes.

→ normalize
EI intensity · EI ÷ size

Total EI ÷ a size metric (usually revenue): impact per dollar of value. Standard ESG term — not a second kind of EI.

01 — Company

Total footprint, then intensity

GHG Protocol builds total EI; intensity divides that total by revenue so peers of different sizes can be compared.

Scope 1
Direct

Owned fuel, fleet, process emissions.

Scope 2
Energy

Purchased electricity, heat, cooling.

Scope 3
Value chain

Up/downstream — often largest & hardest.

EI intensity = total EI ÷ revenue
$$\text{EI intensity} = \frac{\text{Total EI (tCO}_2\text{e)}}{\text{Revenue (\$M)}}$$

Same EI, normalized. Lower = more value per unit of impact. Build EI first: activity × factors (IPCC, EPA).

02 — Investor portfolio

Financed emissions (PCAF)

Aggregate impacts “financed” by loans, equity, and bonds — investor Scope 3 Category 15. Standard: PCAF.

Core formula
$$\text{Financed EI} = \sum (\text{Attribution}_i \times \text{EI of holding } i)$$

Attribution = proportional ownership — not 100% of the investee.

Attribution factor
Listed equity / bonds Outstanding investment ÷ EVIC
Loans / private equity Outstanding ÷ (equity + debt)
Portfolio metrics
Absolute financed tCO₂e Intensity tCO₂e / $M invested WACI weighted avg. intensity
How it’s used
  • Vs. benchmarks & 1.5°C pathways (e.g. PACTA)
  • Portfolio tilt · ISSB / CSRD disclosure
  • Challenges: private data, double-counting, attribution swings
03 — Nation / province

Compare places & track progress

Territorial inventories (UNFCCC, EDGAR, Climate Watch) plus per capita and per GDP; optional consumption-based views.

Comparison metrics
Absolute MtCO₂e Per capita tCO₂e / $GDP Sector + broader EI
Emission index
Base year = 100
$$\frac{E_y}{E_{\text{base}}}\times 100$$

70 vs. base = 30% cut.

Decoupling
Growth vs. emissions
$$\frac{\%\Delta E}{\%\Delta \text{GDP}}$$

<1 or negative = decoupling.

Composite
EPI-style ranks

Multi-indicator scores; peer groups by region / income.

Trajectory
On-track score

Actual path vs. net-zero / NDC pathway.

Used for · watch-outs
  • Policy evaluation · green-growth maps · site / supply-chain choices
  • Data gaps · trade-embodied emissions · index weighting
Company EI → Portfolio EI → Nation / province

Company data feeds PCAF attribution. Firms and portfolios sit in national pathways for policy, benchmarks, and transition risk. Disclose methods · show uncertainty · pair historical + forward-looking metrics.
