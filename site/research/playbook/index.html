What startups need to do now

Lean and strategic. You do not need a multi-million-dollar ESG team — but ignoring metrics can kill enterprise sales and institutional raises.

On this page

  1. Bare minimum
  2. Investor metrics
  3. Physical / lab
  4. Playbook

1. Operational bare minimum (B2B readiness)

If you sell to large enterprises, expect a vendor sustainability questionnaire before signature.

Scope 1 & 2Defensible estimate of direct emissions (often near-zero without fleet/factories) and office/co-working electricity.
Cloud / data center (Scope 3 Cat. 1)For SaaS, this is often the largest line. Buyers ask whether workloads sit in cleaner grid regions.

2. Venture capital & investor metrics

ESG-mandated funds look for operational risk, not perfect data.

Energy intensitykWh per headcount or per revenue — proof the model can scale efficiently.
E-waste policySimple inventory of how hardware is retired and recycled.

3. Hardware, biotech, physical products

Labs and manufacturing cross into regulatory territory regardless of stage.

Hazardous & bio-wasteTrack chemicals/biologicals from intake to licensed haul-away.
Product lifecycleRoHS/REACH-style restricted substances; fix supply chain toxicity at seed, not Series B.

The early-stage playbook

Don’t hire a consultant yet.

  • Use a basic carbon baseline (office, travel, cloud) — Climatico’s free assessment is designed for this.
  • Publish a one-page sustainability statement: baseline + commitment to track as you grow.
  • Host in regions optimized for renewable energy; cloud providers expose regional carbon tools in-console.
