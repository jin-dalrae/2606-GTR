Hiring traditional ESG consultants is incredibly expensive, so having a frictionless, accessible way to verify our green metrics is highly valuable.

Srividiya

Yes, it is frictionless. We don't have to manually input massive spreadsheets of data.

Sam

Dealing with data spreadsheets over the last few months has been a nightmare.

Tej

We are also exploring how we can expand this.

Sam

Our platform is aimed at Gen Z retail investors, typically aged 20 to 35, who find traditional climate investing jargon-heavy, complex, or locked behind high minimums. We are lowering the barrier of entry by allowing users to start investing with as little as $5.

Rae

Do you act as the broker for these transactions?

Sam

Yes, exactly. We are positioning ourselves at a unique intersection. While there have been other attempts in this space, many failed because the market wasn't ready or they didn't execute at the right time. If we were to compare our scale and model to broader platforms, it would be similar to a climate-focused Robinhood, Acorns, or crowdfunding apps. Our direct competitors are startups that offer localized ESG funds, but those are often locked behind high minimums—like $500—and heavy financial jargon. We want to democratize access to these private, early-stage climate projects.

Tej

When you invest, do you receive equity or shares before the company goes IPO?

Sam

These companies will not go IPO for a long time. It functions more like angel investing. You are backing projects you believe in because of their environmental impact, with financial returns acting as a beneficial byproduct rather than the sole driver.

Tej

How do dividends work in that case?

Sam

The projects typically run for 3 to 5 years to achieve their objectives. For example, a biochar project needs to complete several operational phases to succeed. At the end of that period, investors receive returns. Throughout the project, they receive regular impact reports.

Tej

How are shares managed if there is no public market?

Sam

It is structured similarly to a SAFE (Simple Agreement for Future Equity) or angel syndicates. Investors hold a right to future equity or profit-sharing. If the startup generates a profit, dividends are distributed. If they don't, there are no dividends. It is angel investing.

Tej

You are supporting projects that struggle to get traditional funding. Often, they have 50% non-dilutive funding from government grants but need the remaining 50% to execute. Retail investors fill that gap, and once the project succeeds, they receive their returns.

Tej

What framework do you use to evaluate these startups?

Sam

We utilize the Verra Verified Carbon Standard as our baseline. It is the gold standard used by major corporations to verify offsets and footprints. Since early-stage startups don't operate on a massive scale, this baseline works well for our models. Startups work with ESG consultants to verify their metrics, and we translate that data into our standardized baseline to ensure consistency across the platform.

Rae

Let's look at our desktop version.

Tej

Here is the dashboard overview. It shows avoided emissions, emissions trends, and the environmental maturity level of the startup. It also displays 6-month targets, actionable steps, and explains why those steps matter.

Sam

This is highly valuable. It would help us keep track of our environmental metrics in one place without having to manage external spreadsheets and undergo constant reverification.

Tej

How do we keep founders engaged with the dashboard if they are not a pure climate-tech startup, but a traditional tech startup that is simply curious about their footprint?

Sam

If a founder is not naturally climate-conscious, they won't actively seek this out. You can't rely on passive engagement or social-media-style loops. You have to provide clear incentives. For *Sentinel*, we maintain engagement by sending bi-weekly check-ins, asking how things are going, and showing them how optimizing their operations can reduce costs. It functions like customer relationship management.

Tej

In your case, the incentive to maintain accurate metrics on our dashboard could be eligibility to list on your investment platform.

Sam

Exactly. Traditional brokerages and platforms charge massive listing fees, which we do not do. That is a powerful incentive.

Part 5: Monetization & Collaborative Opportunities

Sam

I think there are immense synergies between our B2B model and your B2C platform. We could build a collaborative pipeline. If you onboard startups that want funding but lack verified environmental metrics, they can use your platform to analyze their footprint. Once verified, they can be listed on our investment marketplace. It would create a standardized ecosystem.

Tej

What is the biggest hurdle if we offer this verification service for free? Would founders trust a free tool over a professional consultant?

Sam

Trust and perceived authority are challenges with free tools. People naturally associate high costs with professional quality. If you want to monetize and build trust, you should introduce a tiered pricing model. A basic scorecard can be free, but you charge a reasonable fee—say $50 to $100—for advanced features, such as generating official investor-ready PDF reports. You could also charge a small fee per investor document share. Another highly valuable feature founders would pay for is actionable vendor replacement. If your dashboard tells me to switch to a sustainable supplier and provides their direct contact info and pricing, that saves me hours of research. I would absolutely pay for that kind of direct value.

Tej

So, a sliding scale model based on company size or funding stage?

Sam

Yes, a sliding scale is perfect. For example, $10 for solo founders, and $25 to $50 for teams of 2 to 5. You keep the basic reporting free, but charge for premium, actionable insights and investor-sharing capabilities.

Tej

That makes a lot of sense.
