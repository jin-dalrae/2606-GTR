Climatico product thesis

What we told them we are building, and the ambition beyond a behavior-change dashboard.

Tej

We are currently designing a platform for a dashboard—any kind of software for early-stage startup founders where they can see their environmental impacts, what their startup is bringing on the environment. So instead of focusing solely on money or investment, that can still be a focus, but this can be an add-on for them where they can see all the impacts and also learn how to improve their footprints.

Rae

Because our course is basically about interaction design and experience design, designing the dashboard or application was majorly our focus. But our instructor is urging us to be more courageous — ambitious. Rather than just changing behavior with the platform, they want to see systemic change. A paradigm shift, cultural shift should be there.

Tej

Exactly. The founders use our dashboard to track their handprints and footprints, and plan strategies from that.

Sam

To contrast our platform with yours: we focus on the consumer-retail side, whereas you guys are building for the founder / operator side.

NEEM context (partner lens)

Live product: neem-dusky.vercel.app — “See exactly where your climate money goes.” Pools small contributions into verified carbon removal with open fee splits and project integrity scores. Session notes below explain why their feedback is relevant to Climatico.

Srividiya

It’s a micro-impact investing fintech platform that we’re gearing towards Gen Z and the younger crowd, so that they can put their money towards causes that really matter and see both the impact and the financial returns as well.

Srividiya

Where this idea came from was that I was personally frustrated by the fact that I didn’t have many options to invest in, or that most of the green funds out there were either vague or remote. Even if I do want to support this, my money is still not going to the right cause. So we decided to just build it ourselves.

Sam

We’ve been working on it for about 6 months. We’re in the validation phase right now — interviews with our target audience and some tech founders, figuring out if this is a standalone product or integrated into traditional financial services. We met a venture partner who was interested in the idea as a whole.

Srividiya

I spoke to a lot of founders as well. One of the things that felt like hadn’t been considered as much — and maybe you guys can also jump in — is the exact impact on climate. That’s kind of the main goal of what we’re trying to do.

Srividiya

What we show to our consumers is the impacts on the metrics. We wanted to see if there’s anything in-depth we could go, or if we’re being very vague. I was more interested in doing that exchange.

Why it matters for Climatico: NEEM is the capital / removal-buyer side of climate integrity — same measurement problem, different user. Climatico measures founder ops EI; NEEM shows where pooled climate money goes (site).

Impact metrics & methodology

How footprint is calculated, what standards they use, and how progress is checked over time.

Sam

How are you building the models that you guys use for the footprint? What does that process entail?

Rae

Currently, we are relying on research databases, but we also double-check and run the data through AI-driven models to verify.

Sam

Our models are more… at this point depending on external datasets and standards. We’re not developing anything in-house as of yet. If existing external standards are fine, we might end up using that. If not, we might build our own models as well. As in, what verification methods are already there, like the standards.

Sam

We utilize the Verra Verified Carbon Standard as our baseline. It is the gold standard used by major [climate projects] to evaluate startups / projects we list.

Tej

How do you handle reporting changes in a startup’s environmental performance over time?

Srividiya

We provide status updates on their progress and the methodologies they use. The description details the specific methods they employ to ensure the project remains green and impactful. We plan to check in with them every 6 to 12 months to verify if their metrics are maintaining the baseline or improving.

Tej

So you track their historical data. Do you have features to project future expectations?

Sam

Not yet, not as of now. We haven’t developed metrics for that. While setting future expectations is important, we haven’t built that yet.

Srividiya

Mentor feedback on climate goals UX: give them the steps on how they can improve, and explain why it matters.

Takeaways for Climatico: external standards first; re-verification cadence (6–12 months); “how to improve + why it matters” as product copy; future projections still open.

Founder engagement

How to keep non–climate-first founders coming back to an EI dashboard.

Tej

How do we keep founders engaged with the dashboard if they are not a pure climate-tech startup, but a traditional tech startup that is simply curious about their footprint?

Sam

If a founder is not naturally climate-conscious, they won’t actively seek this out. You can’t rely on passive engagement or social-media-style loops. You have to provide clear incentives. For Sentinel, we maintain engagement by sending bi-weekly check-ins, asking how things are going, and showing them how optimizing their operations can reduce costs. It functions like customer relationship management.

Tej

In your case, the incentive to maintain accurate metrics on our dashboard could be eligibility to list on your investment platform.

Sam

Exactly. Traditional brokerages and platforms charge massive listing fees, which we do not do. That is a powerful incentive.

Takeaways: active CRM-style nudges; tie accuracy to a real benefit (listing, diligence readiness); cost-saving story, not only climate virtue.

Listing criteria & project stages

Stage filters and climate project types on their side — useful for who Climatico should prioritize as a wedge.

Tej

Within your criteria, how small can a company or project be to be listed on your platform?

Sam

I think anything from Seed to Series A. We avoid pre-seed or pre-revenue because listing pre-seed companies on our platform would require us to go through complex regulatory models, and that would function more like a donation at that point since you can’t reliably expect financial returns. So from Seed to Series A that are backed by VCs, or at least have 50% non-dilutive funding.

Tej

Does that include climate projects like grid decarbonization, transport avoidance, and low-carbon materials?

Srividiya

Yes, those are our primary categories.

Sam

Startups, by themselves, are not deeply established in the industry as of now. If we want them to be able to do this [impact measurement], you need to build up that capacity.

Takeaways: Seed–Series A + real capital is a natural Climatico cohort; capacity-building for early teams is the product job, not just a calculator.

Compliance parallel

Their fintech compliance wall is not Climatico’s product, but the pattern maps: “the hard part isn’t building the app.”

Tej

What is the biggest hurdle you see moving forward?

Sam

I think the main challenge that we will face is the compliance and regulatory action that might be taken. That is the main roadblock in our project because building apps and everything is straightforward for me. It’s about how we stay compliant with the financial system while doing this new thing. It’s like Uber in a new generation, but in terms of fintech — the regulatory hurdles are the main thing keeping them at bay.

For Climatico: stay clear of regulated investment advice; lean into measurement + ops decisions. Credibility still comes from standards and transparency, not free-only “vibes.”

Monetization & trust

Direct advice on free tools, pricing tiers, and paid features founders would buy.

Tej

What is the biggest hurdle if we offer this verification service for free? Would founders trust a free tool over a professional consultant?

Sam

Trust and perceived authority are challenges with free tools. People naturally associate high costs with professional quality. If you want to monetize and build trust, you should introduce a tiered pricing model. A basic scorecard can be free, but you charge a reasonable fee—say $50 to $100—for advanced features, such as generating official investor-ready PDF reports. You could also charge a small fee per investor document share. Another highly valuable feature founders would pay for is actionable vendor replacement. If your dashboard tells me to switch to a sustainable supplier and provides their direct contact info and pricing, that saves me hours of research. I would absolutely pay for that kind of direct value.

Tej

So, a sliding scale model based on company size or funding stage?

Sam

Yes, a sliding scale is perfect. For example, $10 for solo founders, and $25 to $50 for teams of 2 to 5. You keep the basic reporting free, but charge for premium, actionable insights and investor-sharing capabilities.

Product priorities they named: free basic scorecard · paid investor-ready PDFs · share fees · vendor replacement with contacts/pricing · sliding scale by team size.

Collaboration pipeline

How Climatico (founder EI) and NEEM (retail climate capital / removal pooling) could feed each other.

Sam

I think there are immense synergies between our B2B model and your B2C platform. We could build a collaborative pipeline. If you onboard startups that want funding but lack verified environmental metrics, they can use your platform to analyze their footprint. Once verified, they can be listed on our investment marketplace. It would create a standardized ecosystem.

Tej

For us, too — because we’re trying to do the same thing: calculating the impact metrics. I wanted to figure out what framework you guys are using and if you could guide us on what work is going to be. So maybe we can collaborate.

Sam

Yeah, absolutely. The Midwest has a hub called the United Innovation House filled with venture partners — a close-knit group. There’s a good possibility that they would support it. I’ve been participating in pitch competitions; it’s been a very positive experience for us.

Srividiya

We have a few friends in the business college who we gave it to, and we asked, “What would you want from a financial aspect of investing in something?” They gave us feedback, and we incorporated it. Then I went to my tech friends and asked, “If you’re into the green tech space, what are you missing?” So we did a survey of about 15 to 20 people from different backgrounds and integrated that.

Shared loop: Climatico measures and improves founder EI → verified metrics unlock capital listing / diligence → capital gate keeps founders honest on the dashboard.
