What Climatebricks is

Climatebricks is a free, modular climate toolkit aimed at early-stage startups — especially teams that want to act on climate without becoming ESG specialists overnight. The product frames climate work as stackable “bricks”: small, concrete building blocks you assemble into a plan that fits your stage, rather than a full corporate inventory on day one.

Public face climatebricks.com · modular climate action for startups
Core metaphor Climate work as LEGO-like bricks — pick, stack, revise — not a one-shot audit package.
Positioning Lower the barrier for founders who care, but lack time, budget, or a sustainability hire.

Who it serves

The wedge is close to Climatico’s: early teams, not Fortune 500 sustainability offices. The bet is that startups freeze architecture, suppliers, and culture early — so the best moment for climate literacy is before lock-in, even if the first steps are imperfect.

Typical user Founder / ops lead who needs a path, not a 200-page protocol.
Job-to-be-done “Help me start climate action with my team, in language I understand, without buying an enterprise ESG suite.”

How it works (the brick idea)

Climatebricks treats climate maturity as a composition problem. Instead of forcing a single mega-workflow (“complete your full Scope 1–3 inventory”), it offers discrete modules — bricks — such as measurement starting points, operational changes, policy / culture moves, and narrative for customers or investors. Teams pick what is relevant now and leave the rest for later.

Why modularity lands Startups have uneven data (cloud bills yes; supplier primary factors no). Bricks match partial reality.
Why “plan” language lands Founders already think in roadmaps and sprints. Climate becomes another backlog of actions, not a guilt report.

Why it’s an important reference for Climatico

Climatico is building Environmental Impact (EI) for early-stage founders — estimate, intensity, evidence, and a path to better decisions. Climatebricks is not a twin product, but it proves several product truths Climatico depends on:

1. Early-stage is a real product segment

Enterprise tools (Normative, Persefoni-class platforms) and free EPA Excel both miss the “we are five people on a Series Seed cloud stack” user. Climatebricks shows the market already recognizes that gap. Climatico can own the measurement + decision layer of that gap — EI intensity, hotspots, scenarios — while Climatebricks-style modularity informs how we stage the journey.

2. Modularity beats “complete the form”

Climatico fieldwork and founder sessions keep repeating the same pain: spreadsheets, vague green funds, and tools that demand data startups do not have. A brick metaphor is a design pattern for progressive disclosure: start with what you can know (cloud, travel, office), then grow into Scope 3 and handprint as evidence matures. That is exactly how Climatico’s integrity gates and maturity path should feel.

3. Action is the product, not only a footprint number

Climatebricks positions climate work as things you do, not only a number you report. Climatico’s actionable-insights poster and GTR Partners dashboard story push the same idea: hotspots, scenarios, vendor switches, investor-ready packs. Climatebricks is a reference for action packaging — name the next brick, not just the tonne total.

4. Language that founders will actually open

“Bricks” is plain, slightly playful, and non-threatening. Climatico’s ledger / tabloid voice can stay serious about integrity while still learning from tools that avoid ESG jargon. If the first screen feels like compliance theater, early founders bounce — Climatebricks is a reminder that tone is a retention feature.

5. Free entry, paid depth (market pattern)

Climatebricks sits in the freemium / free-start climate-tool landscape. Our collaboration notes with NEEM founders and our market note both say the same thing: free builds trust only if depth is real and premium value is clear (investor PDFs, vendor replacement, shareable packs). Climatebricks is a benchmark for how low the on-ramp can be — and for where Climatico must go further on defensible numbers.

Design takeaways for Climatico

Stage the product as bricks Onboarding → first intensity estimate → hotspot brick → scenario brick → investor pack. Never “finish GHG Protocol” on week one.
Name the next action Every screen should answer “what do I change this week?” Climatebricks wins on action framing; we win if action is tied to EI math.
Keep the on-ramp free and honest Free scorecard + clear uncertainty. Charge for depth (share packs, vendor swaps, cohort boards) — same freemium pattern, higher integrity bar.
Cite the pattern, don’t clone the brand Climatebricks is a competitor-adjacent reference in the early-stage climate tool space. Use it to justify modular UX research; differentiate on measurement + capital language.
