Why startups need to care

Not because every seed company needs an ESG department — because the choices you make now become expensive defaults later.

On this page

  1. Lock-in
  2. Procurement wall
  3. Capital risk

Managing future impact from day one is critical for three unromantic reasons. (This chapter restates the foundations for founder-facing decks and fieldwork scripts.)

1. Structural technology lock-in

Cloud region, architecture, and supplier choices set an emission trajectory that scales with product usage. Moving later is a logistics and eng-hours tax.

2. Upstream Scope 3 procurement wall

Enterprise buyers under CSRD, SB 253, and SBTi-style targets treat vendors as Scope 3 line items. Carbon-heavy products struggle at procurement, not just at PR.

3. Decoupling carbon from capital

If emissions track revenue 1:1, you carry transition risk into every raise. Early intensity tracking is how founders prove the model can grow cleaner, not just bigger.

GTR stanceEvery business has impact — good or bad. Climate-tech or not. The job is to make that impact legible by time and by growth, then improve it.
