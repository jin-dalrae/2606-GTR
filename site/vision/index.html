Next vision · Imagination

If this works, the scoreboard changes.

Today we help one startup see footprint and handprint. Tomorrow, if GTR succeeds, impact intensity becomes as legible as revenue — in boardrooms, term sheets, and public culture.

Three shifts

System. Culture. Paradigm. Nested layers of the same bet.

01

System change

Climate data stops living in annual PDF reports for the largest companies only. Every venture-backed firm ships a living ledger: evidence in, estimates out, maturity scored, handprint claims gated on additionality. Investors allocate capital with portfolio impact boards — not gut feel after a pitch.

02

Culture change

Founders brag about impact ratios the way they brag about ARR and burn multiple. Hiring pages show “tCO₂e per $1M revenue” next to remote policy. Customers ask for intensity the way they ask for security questionnaires. Green claims without evidence become socially expensive, not just legally risky.

03

Paradigm shift

Competition expands beyond revenue and GDP. Nations, cities, funds, and companies race on environmental impact ratios — value created per tonne, harm avoided per product unit, handprint integrity under audit. Growth that cannot decouple from damage is no longer celebrated as success.

Scenes from a world that measures what it values

Small stories of system, culture, and paradigm landing in everyday life.

Near term

The diligence tab opens first

A partner does not start with the growth chart. They open the GTR share link: footprint hotspots, maturity level, additionality status on handprint claims, and the stranding-year countdown if the architecture does not change. The raise still depends on product — but climate is no longer a slide-28 afterthought.

Medium term

Founders compete on ratio

Demo days include a second scoreboard: environmental impact ratio vs peers at the same stage. Not a beauty contest of “net zero someday,” but tCO₂e per revenue, per FTE, and verified avoided tonnes — with red flags for ungated handprints. Status accrues to those who improve the ratio while scaling.

Long arc

GDP shares the stage

Cities and countries publish growth alongside impact intensity the way they publish unemployment. Capital markets price transition risk in continuous signals, not annual PDFs. “Successful” means creating value that does not mortgage the climate commons — and tools like GTR are infrastructure for that accounting culture, not a niche climate app.
