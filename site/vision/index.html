The core idea

Company-level EI calculation and impact intensity — elaborated from the concept thread that drives this vision.

Company-wise environmental impact

Every firm gets a living ledger: footprint (−) from operations and value chain, handprint (+) only when additionality holds, maturity scored L0–L5, claims gated on evidence. Same language GTR prototypes today — Scope-aware, sourced factors, modeled until metered.

Not a one-off consultant PDF. A continuous operator surface: evidence in, estimates out, first action binding to the next maturity step.

Intensity, not vibes

EI / Revenue
Environmental impact per unit of economic output

Pair absolute tonnes (tCO₂e/yr) with intensity (tCO₂e per $1M revenue, per FTE, per unit shipped). Growth without a better ratio is no longer automatic success.

Three shifts

System. Culture. Paradigm. Nested layers of the same EI/Revenue bet.

01

System change

Climate data stops living in annual PDF reports for the largest companies only. Every venture-backed firm ships a company-wise EI ledger: evidence in, estimates out, maturity scored, handprint claims gated on additionality. Investors open portfolio boards ranked by intensity — not gut feel after a pitch.

02

Culture change

Founders brag about EI / Revenue the way they brag about ARR and burn multiple. Hiring pages show “tCO₂e per $1M revenue” next to remote policy. Customers ask for intensity the way they ask for security questionnaires. Green claims without evidence become socially expensive, not just legally risky.

03

Paradigm shift

Competition expands beyond revenue and GDP. Nations, cities, funds, and companies race on environmental impact ratios — value created per tonne, harm avoided per product unit, handprint integrity under audit. Growth that cannot decouple from damage is no longer celebrated as success.

Scenes from a world that measures what it values

From one company’s EI ledger to a public resource index.

Near term

Company-wise EI is the diligence tab

A partner does not start with the growth chart. They open the GTR share link: company-wise footprint hotspots, EI/Revenue vs peers, maturity level, additionality status on handprint claims, and the stranding-year countdown if the architecture does not change. Climate is no longer slide-28.

Medium term

Founders compete on EI / Revenue

Demo days include a second scoreboard: environmental impact ratio vs peers at the same stage. Not “net zero someday,” but tCO₂e per revenue, per FTE, and verified avoided tonnes — with red flags for ungated handprints. Status accrues to those who improve the ratio while scaling.

Long arc

National index on the air

Cities and countries broadcast growth alongside resource-depletion intensity the way they broadcast unemployment. Capital markets price transition risk in continuous signals, not annual PDFs. GTR-like ledgers become the company-level sensors feeding that national index — infrastructure, not a niche app.

“What if the companies we celebrate are not only the ones that grow the fastest — but the ones that grow with the lightest scar, the strongest handprint, and a falling EI / Revenue?”
