Footprint (−)

Emissions you cause — Scope 1, 2, and material Scope 3. Defaults cite GHG Protocol, SCI, DEFRA, EPA, Project Frame.

B

Handprint (+)

Avoided emissions your product enables — only when additionality holds. Never a substitute for certified accounting.

Any business model

04 — If this works

The scoreboard changes.

Start with company-wise environmental impact. Then EI / Revenue as the competitive ratio. A dashboard for operators. A board for portfolios. Then a culture — and a national index — that treats intensity as public infrastructure.

Company-wise environmental impact

Every firm gets a living ledger: footprint (−) from operations and value chain, handprint (+) only when additionality holds, maturity scored L0–L5, claims gated on evidence. Same language Climatico prototypes today — Scope-aware, sourced factors, modeled until metered.

Not a one-off consultant PDF. A continuous operator surface: evidence in, estimates out, first action binding to the next maturity step.

Intensity, not vibes

EI / Revenue
Environmental impact per unit of economic output

Pair absolute tonnes (tCO₂e/yr) with intensity (tCO₂e per $1M revenue, per FTE, per unit shipped). Growth without a better ratio is no longer automatic success.

05 — Three shifts

System. Culture. Paradigm.

01

System change

Climate data stops living in annual PDF reports for the largest companies only. Every venture-backed firm ships a company-wise EI ledger: evidence in, estimates out, maturity scored, handprint claims gated on additionality. Investors open portfolio boards ranked by intensity — not gut feel after a pitch.

02

Culture change

Founders brag about EI / Revenue the way they brag about ARR and burn multiple. Hiring pages show “tCO₂e per $1M revenue” next to remote policy. Customers ask for intensity the way they ask for security questionnaires. Green claims without evidence become socially expensive — environmentally accountable capitalism.

03

Paradigm shift

Competition expands beyond revenue and GDP. Nations, cities, funds, and companies race on environmental impact ratios — value created per tonne, harm avoided per product unit, handprint integrity under audit. Growth that cannot decouple from damage is no longer celebrated as success.

07 — Scenes

From one company’s EI ledger to a public resource index.

Near term

Company-wise EI is the diligence tab

A partner does not start with the growth chart. They open the Climatico share link: company-wise footprint hotspots, EI/Revenue vs peers, maturity level, additionality status on handprint claims, and the stranding-year countdown if the architecture does not change. Climate is no longer slide-28.

Medium term

Founders compete on EI / Revenue

Demo days include a second scoreboard: environmental impact ratio vs peers at the same stage. Not “net zero someday,” but tCO₂e per revenue, per FTE, and verified avoided tonnes — with red flags for ungated handprints. Status accrues to those who improve the ratio while scaling.

Long arc

National index on the air

Cities and countries broadcast growth alongside resource-depletion intensity the way they broadcast unemployment. Capital markets price transition risk in continuous signals, not annual PDFs. Climatico-like ledgers become the company-level sensors feeding that national index — infrastructure, not a niche app.

“What if the companies we celebrate are not only the ones that grow the fastest — but the ones that grow with the lightest scar, the strongest handprint, and a falling EI / Revenue?”
