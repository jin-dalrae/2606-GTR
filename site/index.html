01 — Why day one

Startups scale fast. The window to intervene is early.

Large companies must report ESG and emissions. Early-stage startups usually do not — so impact is easy to ignore until infrastructure, suppliers, and cloud regions are already locked in.

The gap

No obligation — yet

Enterprises face disclosure and procurement pressure. Pre-seed and seed teams rarely measure cloud, travel, or manufacturing at all.

The risk

Lock-in as you grow

One wrong factory, grid, or stack choice compounds with every user. Fixing it at Series B costs engineering years you do not have.

The move

Intervene while plastic

Estimate Environmental Impact (EI) from the idea stage. Cascade by funding period. Change the trajectory before scale multiplies it.

02 — Environmental Impact (EI)

Not emotional intelligence. Not vibes. A living score.

We always spell it out on first use: EI means Environmental Impact — absolute footprint, then intensity so a tiny company is not “clean” only because it is small.

EI

Absolute Environmental Impact — how much you emit (tCO₂e today; water, energy, waste in the longer model). Past, present, and a forward estimate as the business scales.

EI intensity

EI divided by revenue (or growth). The ratio that matters when you scale: are you getting cleaner per dollar, or just bigger?

Footprint (−) is what you cause. Handprint (+) is avoided impact only when additionality holds — never a free offset. Modeled estimates until you replace them with real ops data.

06 — If this works

Environmentally accountable capitalism.

From one company’s EI ledger, to cohort competition, to capital that prices intensity — growth without a better ratio is no longer automatic success.

System · culture · paradigm

System — climate data leaves annual PDFs; every venture-backed firm keeps a living EI surface, and accelerators embed it through demo day.

Culture — founders brag about EI intensity the way they brag about ARR; customers and hires ask for it.

Paradigm — funds and regions compete on value created per tonne of impact, not revenue alone.

The competitive ratio

EI / Revenue
Environmental Impact per unit of output

Absolute tonnes still matter. Intensity is how you stay honest while you scale — and how portfolios and cohorts compare without drowning in raw tables.

“What if the companies we celebrate are not only the ones that grow the fastest — but the ones that grow with the lightest scar, and a falling EI intensity?”
