Two startups, two very different climate stories

Your footprint hides where you least expect it. Here's what our assessment surfaces for founders like you.

Verba AI

RAG SaaS · Seed

The business: A B2B assistant that answers employees' questions over a company's internal docs using retrieval-augmented generation. Revenue is per-seat plus usage; the product runs continuous GPU inference and a vector database in the cloud.

Where their impact lands

Almost everything is Scope 2 & 3 cloud compute — GPU inference electricity plus the embodied carbon of data-center hardware. A 12-person team can carry the compute footprint of a mid-size factory, and it grows with every query.

What's forcing their hand

The EU AI Act (GPAI energy & resource-use disclosure, live Aug 2025) and customer pressure from CSRD and California SB 253: enterprise buyers must report Scope 3, so they demand per-workload emissions from Verba before signing.

The unexpected ripple

Jevons rebound. When Verba ships a cheaper, faster model tier, customers 10× their query volume — total inference emissions rise even as per-query efficiency improves. It typically shows up 2–4 quarters after a price cut, and spikes again the week a big customer rolls out company-wide.

Trottr

Pet services · Series A

The business: A marketplace for fresh, refrigerated pet-food delivery and on-demand dog walking. Subscription meals are made from real meat, shipped cold to the door, with a gig network of walkers.

Where their impact lands

Footprint is dominated by Scope 3 livestock ingredients (beef & chicken), cold-chain delivery, and packaging waste — not the dog-walking app everyone thinks of. Refrigerant leakage from delivery vans is a hidden Scope 1 source.

What's forcing their hand

The EU Deforestation Regulation (EUDR) — in force for large operators since 30 Dec 2025 — requires beef and soy to be traced as deforestation-free. Add PPWR packaging rules and the F-Gas Regulation HFC phase-down on refrigerated transport.

The unexpected ripple

A supply-chain cliff. EUDR just hit — their meat supply chain is suddenly non-compliant, forcing origin traceability within months. Then in 2027, tightening F-gas quotas surface van-refrigerant leaks as the single largest line item they never measured.

How is this calculated?

Each activity you selected maps to a typical annual emission factor (tCO2e/yr) drawn from climate-tech startup benchmarks, scaled to your stage and model. We sum these into your footprint and propagate each factor's uncertainty in quadrature (root-sum-of-squares) to produce the ± range.

    Inside the dashboard you replace any modeled factor with metered or metered-equivalent data, which shrinks the uncertainty and raises your verified-data coverage.
