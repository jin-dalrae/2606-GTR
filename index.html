Methodology

How the assessment is modeled

The first report is a directional model. It is designed to surface the likely hotspots and evidence gaps fast, then improve as you replace defaults with measured data.

Built for Seed–Series B climate-tech founders modeling their first investor-grade snapshot. Not built for mature enterprise sustainability teams that already have audited Scope 1–3 data, or for pre-revenue hobby projects without a clear operating footprint.

01

Context

We use your company profile, business model, team size, website text, founder notes, and selected activities to understand the operating situation.

02

Footprint Model

Each activity maps to a modeled annual emission factor in tCO2e, grouped across Scope 1, Scope 2, and Scope 3 categories.

03

Uncertainty

Every modeled factor carries an uncertainty range. The report combines those ranges with root-sum-of-squares propagation.

04

Verification

After login, the dashboard turns the preview into goals, Risk Radar items, evidence gaps, and a path to replace estimates with metered sources.

Detailed Factor Methodology

Public model

These are the default factors available to the instant assessment before measured data is connected. A company-specific report shows only the activities selected for that startup.

  • Inputs: company profile, business model, team size, website context, founder notes, and selected activities.
  • Evidence level: defaults are modelled estimates until replaced with metered cloud, utility, travel, vendor, logistics, or source-document data.
  • Uncertainty: every modelled factor carries an uncertainty range that propagates into the footprint total.

          💡 Footprint & Handprint Verification Guide

          Physical Footprint (−)

          Emissions your company physically causes. Goal: replace modeled estimates with metered data.

          • Cloud Compute: Replace default calculations with real emissions telemetry from your AWS/GCP/Azure sustainability dashboards.
          • FTE Travel: Replace flat estimates with actual flight segments, passenger class, and hotel room nights.
          • General Waste: Replace defaults with actual waste bin counts, organic compost records, or recycling manifests.
          Avoided Handprint (+)

          Emissions avoided by customers choosing you. Goal: verify additionality and baseline math.

          • Establish the Baseline: Document the conventional alternative (e.g. diesel delivery vans, coal grid energy, virgin materials).
          • Pass Additionality Gates: Prove the carbon reduction isn't already mandated by regulations or happening anyway.
          • Track Displacement: Multiply verified avoided emissions per unit sold by your actual shipment volume.
